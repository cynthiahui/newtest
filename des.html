Investment Types

Stocks

What it is: When you buy a stock, you’re buying a tiny piece of a company. If the company does well, the value of your stock can go up, and you can make money. If the company does poorly, the value of your stock can go down.

Risk: High (the value can go up and down a lot).

Potential Return: High (if the company grows a lot).

Bonds

What it is: A bond is like a loan that you give to a government or company. They promise to pay you back with interest (extra money) after a certain time.

Risk: Low (because the government or company promises to pay you back).

Potential Return: Low (but steady and safe).

Mutual Funds

What it is: A mutual fund is a big collection of stocks, bonds, or other investments. You and many other people invest money together to buy a bunch of things. This helps you spread out your risk.

Risk: Medium (because it’s spread out, but some things might still lose value).

Potential Return: Medium (it’s a mix of safe and risky investments).

ETFs (Exchange-Traded Funds)

What it is: An ETF is kind of like a mutual fund, but it trades on the stock market like a stock. You buy shares of it, and it holds a lot of different investments (like stocks or bonds).

Risk: Medium (it’s a mix, so it’s less risky than individual stocks).

Potential Return: Medium (because it’s made up of many things).
